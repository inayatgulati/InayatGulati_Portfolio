Overview

Project Overview

I specialised in developing the interactive elements for the Isola Design District microsite — creating an engaging digital platform that enhances exploration of Milan's vibrant design district while promoting local tourism and art exhibitions.

Microsite
Interactive digital experience
An intuitive interface where visitors could navigate the district, discover exhibitions, and plan their route — all in one place.
Poster
Print-to-digital bridge
A graphic design system that mirrored the microsite's visual language — creating a seamless transition between physical and digital touchpoints.
Isola
Milan's design district
The Isola neighbourhood during Fuorisalone — one of Milan's most vibrant creative hubs with exhibitions, installations, and local venues.
Mission
Wayfinding + discovery
Create an engaging digital platform that enhances exploration of the district while promoting local tourism and art exhibitions to festival visitors.

Project Goals

Six Goals, One Brief

Our brief from Fuorisalone set six clear goals — spanning wayfinding, culture, and seamless cross-medium experience design.

01
Intuitive wayfinding system
Create a navigation experience that guides district visitors naturally through Isola's streets and exhibition spaces.
02
Showcase local art exhibitions and venues
Surface the rich programme of exhibitions and local attractions within the district in an engaging, browseable format.
03
Encourage exploration
Design an experience that sparks curiosity and motivates visitors to move through the district and discover unexpected spaces.
04
Integrate navigation with interactive elements
Combine practical guide map functionality with previews, distances, and venue information in a single cohesive interface.
05
Represent the district's creative culture
Reflect Isola's vibrant, design-forward identity through both the print poster and the digital microsite.
06
Seamless print-to-digital transition
Ensure the poster and microsite speak the same visual language — a unified experience across both mediums.

Integration Strategy

One Visual Language, Two Mediums

The integration strategy ensured visitors experienced Discover Isola as a single cohesive world — whether encountering the poster first or arriving directly at the microsite.

✦
Animatable poster elements
Every visual element in the poster was designed with digital animation in mind — shapes, bars, and grids that could move and respond to user interaction.
🎨
Vibrant colour consistency
The pink, purple, and lime green palette remained consistent and legible across both ink-on-paper and screen — tested across multiple contexts and light conditions.
📖
Readability across formats
Typography choices were validated for legibility against vibrant backgrounds in both print and on-screen contexts.
⊞
Flexible grid system
The underlying grid structure flexed between the fixed poster format and the dynamic, responsive layout of the microsite without breaking visual coherence.

Challenges

Three Challenge Areas

The project spanned visual, technical, and design balance challenges — each requiring a different kind of problem-solving.

Visual Integration
🖼
Finding the right imagery
Sourcing photography that authentically captured Isola's neighbourhood character and Milan's broader design culture — without feeling generic or touristic.
🔗
Coherent visual language across formats
Creating a design system that worked equally well as static ink on paper and as animated, interactive components on screen — a constraint that shaped every element.
👁
Text contrast on vibrant backgrounds
Maintaining readable typography against the pink, purple, and lime green palette required careful contrast testing across both print and digital colour profiles.
Technical Implementation
⚡
Smooth interaction transitions
Developing fluid transitions between different interactive states — map views, venue previews, distance calculations — without jarring jumps or loading delays.
🗺
Guide map + location previews
Integrating GIS-based map functionality with real-time location previews required careful coordination between geographic data layers and the visual interface.
📱
Cross-device performance
Ensuring consistent performance and visual fidelity across different devices and screen sizes — from desktop browsers to mobile phones used during festival navigation.
Design Balance
🎨
Harmonising the vibrant palette
Applying a high-energy colour system across all elements without creating visual fatigue or reducing the legibility of practical information.
✨
Engaging without overwhelming
Balancing rich interactions and visual interest with clarity of purpose — visitors needed to find exhibitions and navigate efficiently, not just admire the interface.
⊞
Hierarchy with dynamic elements
Maintaining clear visual hierarchy while incorporating animated and interactive components — ensuring the most important information always read first.
