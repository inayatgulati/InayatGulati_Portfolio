Overview

The Project

During my UX/UI internship at Mastercard's Vancouver Tech Hub, I worked within the Workplace Experience Department under the VP of Workplace Experience.

My main focus was redesigning the internal SharePoint platform — transforming it from a static information repository into a comprehensive user guide system. The redesign served the Vancouver office primarily, while incorporating feedback from global teams to ensure relevance for visiting and transferring employees.

The platform consolidated everything from office policies, catering services, and facility booking forms to travel information and event resources — so every employee could navigate workplace processes without friction.

Ideation & Design Process

Four Key
Design Decisions

Collaborating with the VP and Project Manager, I synthesised research into clear design directions — then validated them with teams in Dublin, India, and the US.

🗂
Decision 1 — Modular homepage with side navigation
Designed a structured side navigation menu with key categories (Home, Visiting Vancouver, Transportation, Amenities, Events, Floor Plans, Forms & Policies) — replacing the scattered layout so employees could find what they needed within seconds.
🏗
Decision 2 — Information architecture overhaul
Restructured the entire information hierarchy based on user goals rather than departmental ownership. New categories like "Plan Your Visit," "Book a Space," and "Workplace Support" reflected how employees naturally searched for information.
🎨
Decision 3 — Visual identity + accessibility
Established a refined colour and layout system: black and off-white for sub-categories, black for all interactive buttons (strong contrast, accessibility compliant), muted grey for circulars, bright orange for notices. Enhanced scannability and brand consistency across every page.
🌐
Decision 4 — Global scalability
Shared Figma prototypes async with teams in Dublin, India, and the US for feedback. The final framework was flexible enough to accommodate local content while maintaining consistent IA and design language across all global instances.

Reflection

What I Learned

Enterprise UX taught me things no classroom project could — about constraints, stakeholders, and shipping work at real scale.

Designing for global teams
I learned how to create systems flexible enough to work across countries, cultures, and time zones — balancing regional specificity with consistent information architecture.
Strategic thinking at scale
Journey mapping and persona development helped me tie every UX decision directly to employee pain points and organisational goals — making stakeholder buy-in straightforward.
Adaptability in constraints
Designing within SharePoint's technical framework taught me to balance creativity with feasibility — an essential skill for enterprise UX that you can't learn from Figma alone.
Leadership without a title
As the only designer, I had to communicate, advocate, and make informed design decisions confidently — often translating design rationale for non-design stakeholders in real time.
